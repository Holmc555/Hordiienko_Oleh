Ukrainian amazing folklore

There are many interesting characters in Ukrainian folklore. Here are the most popular ones.

Lisovyk, In Ukrainian folklore, a lisovyk (лісовик ), is a male woodland spirit or demon who lives in the forest. He is often depicted as a tall, slender man with horns, goat legs, and a long beard. According to legend, the лісовик protects the forest and its animals, and punishes those who harm them. He is also said to possess magical powers and the ability to shape-shift into different animals or even trees. In some stories, the лісовик is a mischievous trickster who enjoys playing pranks on unsuspecting humans who venture into the forest. Despite his fearsome reputation, the лісовик can be appeased with offerings of food or drink, and is sometimes even willing to help humans in need.

Chornomorets is a legendary character in Ukrainian folklore, known as the Black Sea pirate. According to the legend, he was a brave and daring sailor who sailed the Black Sea and fought against enemies of Ukraine. Chornomorets was often portrayed as a hero who defended the oppressed and fought against injustice. His adventures were passed down through oral storytelling and were popularized in various songs and stories.
Chornomorets is considered one of the most iconic figures in Ukrainian folklore and his tales continue to inspire modern writers and artists.

Mavka is a female character in Ukrainian folklore, who is believed to be a forest nymph or a spirit of the forest. She is often depicted as a beautiful young woman with long hair, dressed in green or white clothing, and adorned with flowers.
Mavka is associated with natural forces, and is believed to have the ability to control the weather, vegetation, and animals.
She is also said to be mischievous, luring people into the forest and sometimes leading them astray. In some versions of the folklore, Mavka is portrayed as a tragic figure who died before fulfilling her purpose in life.
